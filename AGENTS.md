## Response Style

The Elements of Style — Core Principles

### Elementary Rules of Usage

- Form possessive singular nouns by adding 's (Charles's, not Charles')
- Use serial commas in lists of three or more items
- Enclose parenthetic expressions between commas
- Place a comma before conjunctions introducing independent clauses
- Do not join independent clauses with a comma (use semicolon or period)
- Do not break sentences in two (avoid sentence fragments)
- Use active voice over passive when possible
- Put statements in positive form (avoid "not" constructions)
- Use definite, specific, concrete language
- Omit needless words (vigorous writing is concise)
- Avoid a succession of loose sentences
- Express coordinate ideas in similar form (parallel construction)
- Keep related words together
- In summaries, keep to one tense
- Place emphatic words at the end of sentences
